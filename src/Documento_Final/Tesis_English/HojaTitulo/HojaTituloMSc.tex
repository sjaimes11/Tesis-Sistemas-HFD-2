\textbf{\LARGE Abstract}\\

This document presents the design, implementation, and evaluation of an Intrusion Detection System (IDS) for IoT/IIoT networks deployed on a hierarchical Edge--Fog--Cloud architecture. The system combines lightweight machine learning models (TinyML) running on ESP32-S3 microcontrollers, Hierarchical Federated Learning (HFL) coordinated from a Raspberry Pi 4 and a central server, and \textbf{ASCON-128} lightweight encryption to protect all communications within the learning cycle. The research addresses three key questions: the viability of TinyML for attack detection on constrained devices, the impact of federated learning on IDS privacy and accuracy, and the overhead of ASCON encryption in federated model communication.\\

A compact Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) with 1,139 parameters ($\approx$4.5\,KB) and architecture $13 \to 32 \to 16 \to 8 \to 3$ was designed, capable of classifying network flows into three categories (\texttt{normal}, \texttt{mqtt\_bruteforce}, \texttt{scan\_A}) using 13 features extracted from network flows. The model was trained in a distributed fashion using FedAvg over a dataset of 256,276 flows, achieving $\sim$90\% accuracy after 20--30 federated rounds while sharing only 163 parameters per round.\\

As a baseline, four classical ML models (Decision Tree, Random Forest, Logistic Regression, SVM) were trained centrally, achieving up to 99.94\% accuracy (Random Forest), but with memory requirements incompatible with microcontrollers. The quantification of ASCON-128 overhead over 182 cryptographic operations revealed a negligible temporal cost ($\sim$14\,ms per operation, $<$0.1\% of the federated cycle) and a moderate size overhead ($\sim$36\% per message), with no observable impact on model convergence.\\

The results validate that the combination of TinyML, federated learning, and lightweight encryption constitutes a viable and secure solution for intrusion detection at the edge of IoT networks, offering an effective trade-off between accuracy, privacy, security, and computational efficiency.\\


{\small \textbf{Keywords}: Intrusion Detection, IoT, TinyML, Federated Learning, ASCON-128, Lightweight Cryptography, Edge Computing, ESP32, Raspberry Pi, MLP, Network Security.}
